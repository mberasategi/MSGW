Preparation

You will get the most out of this class if you (1) attend class, (2) complete all the readings, and (3) engage11 Take detailed notes, watch the videos and supplementary content, work through the examples given in the textbook, have vivid dreams about them, etc. with the readings2.2 Also (4) ask for help or clarifications if you have doubts!

You will be graded on your attendance and preparation through regular self-reporting backed by the honor system.33 Be honest! At the beginning of class once a week, I will post a quiz on ALUD with the following questions:

  1. How much of this week's reading did you finish?
    • 100% (5 points)
    • 75–99% (4 points)
    • 50–74% (3 points)
    • 11–49% (1 points)
    • 0–10% (0 points)
  2. How well did you read?
    • I was engaged and read carefully (5 points)
    • I was fairly engaged and read fairly carefully (4 points)
    • I skimmed it (2 points)
    • I didn’t read it at all (0 points)
  3. Did you listen to a podcast episode this week?
    • Yes (2 points)
    • No (0 points)

Each week is worth 12 points. It is unlikely that you’ll score a 12 every week.44 But it would be amazing if you did!

Online journalism news

One of the objectives of this class is to be up to date with current advances in online journalism, not only in terms of technologies, but also regarding trends, formats, etc.

There is an individual weekly assignment that will help with this, consisting on two distinct tasks:

Listen to podcasts about online journalism

The course materials section of the syllabus explains that you need to listen to at least one episode of an online journalism-related podcast every week. These will allow you to be up to date in current debates regarding tech, new formats, and other kinds of trends in online journalism.

You will then write a short piece, intended to be published online55 So try to apply the things you already know about this, and what you will (hopefully) learn during the semester, in your writing., about the listening you have made for the week. You could just review the podcast you have listened to, or you could write about the underlying topic they handle using them as your main source. Just have in mind that this should be written to be publishable in a blog, for example.

You will post this piece of writing to the corresponding assignment in ALUD66 Make sure you are aware of specific requirements and deadlines for this. and, sometimes, selected pieces will also be actually published in Infocom, the digital magazine by Communication students.

Peer-review other contributions

In a second stage for this weekly task, once the deadline for submissions has been met, you will be peer-reviewing contributions by classmates according to a set rubric. You will automatically be assigned three other contributions (which may or may not be dealing with the same sources you used — this is irrelevant) and you will need to fill-in a rubric for each.

Your grade for this individual weekly assignment will be mainly taken from the reviews from your classmates, but part will also correspond to the level of agreement of your reviews of other contributions: this is a measure to prevent individuals being too benign or too strict when evaluating others, as extreme positions in grading get penalized.7 So be honest and reasonable when filling in the rubrics.
