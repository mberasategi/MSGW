Finding leads and sources online

Highlights

When anyone can seek out information about topical issues online, advanced search skills become a must for professional journalists. (p.70)

Search engines

  • Rank content on how many other pages link to it
    → unlikely that you will find ‘exclusive’ or suprising information by default
  • Some srategies to fine-tune search results:
    • specialist jargon, codes, specific names (p.61)
    • vertical search engines (p.63), which focus on more specific fields
    • search operators (p.59)

Search engines: search operators

 

Most people use search engines by searching for what they want to know. However, to use them more effectively you should describe what you expect to find. (p.59)

Search engines: search operators

  • site:, by specifying the domain that published the information
  • filetype:, by limiting to particular types of documents
  • inurl:, intitle:
  • exclude with -, useful to overcome ambiguities
  • wildcards like *, to leave some room to abiguity
  • time range, when it was published
  • cache, also available through the WaybackMachine

Social networks

  • Lists allow to filter/classify accounts depending on different criteria
  • Dashboards improve the interface to access social networks
  • Search John Espirian (2018), Twitter Search Advanced Guide

Finding people

  • Experts: insiders, academics, observers, campaigners…
    • Find research first, as a way to contact the researcher
    • People search through LinkedIn
  • Case studies
  • People on the scene

Finding people

 

These techniques should be attempted alongside analogous approaches in the physical world, such as contacting organisations representing people […], or physically going to relevant locations[, or picking up the phone]. (p.66)

Alerts and RSS feeds

There are two main techniques that are used for a personal news feed: email alerts and RSS feeds. (p.52)

  • Google Alerts to email. Search operators may be used, and then get emailed every time new results are found.
  • RSS Feeds allow to receive a feed of information from a specific source. Feed readers comgine different RSS feeds into a single view.

Alerts and RSS feeds

  • These two can be combined — Google Alerts also provide RSS output (instead of email)
  • Further automation is not only possible, but also easy, thanks to

Jenna Wortham (2011), 7 Ways to Automate your Life with IFTTT

Abigail Edge (2014), 10 New IFTTT Recipes for Journalists and Newsrooms
